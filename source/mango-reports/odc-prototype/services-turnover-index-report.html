Activity
The combination of actions that result in a certain set of products. An activity can be said to take place when resources such as equipment, labour, manufacturing techniques or products are combined, leading to specific goods or services. Thus, an activity is characterised by an input of resources, a production process and an output of products.
Turnover index
It is the objective of the turnover index to show the development of the market for goods and services.
Turnover (1) comprises the totals invoiced by the observation unit during the reference period, and this corresponds to market sales of goods or services supplied to third parties. Turnover also includes all other charges (transport, packaging, etc.) passed on to the customer, even if these charges are listed separately in the invoice. (...)

Last update at Eurostat side was performed on the 2nd of June 2011.

Data sources

The following datasets have been combined in order to produce the displayed figures:
  • Services activities turnover index have been extracted from Eurostat's "Other services turnover index - annual data" dataset - bulk download facility - (sts_setu_a). This dataset is described in the corresponding metadata document of Eurostat.
  • Definitions of terms Activity, and turnover index have been extracted from RAMON, Eurostat's metadata server.

Data processing

Following filtering and grouping tasks have been performed in order to kept the volume of data presented reasonable:
  • Countries have been filtered (only EU27 countries have been kept).
  • Activities have been filtered, only eight activities have been kept. Activities classification is described here: Eurostat NACE R2 classification.
  • Countries have been grouped into regions according to the United Nations geoscheme.

Footnotes have not been kept.

This report displays one measure: turnover index, according to three dimensions:

  • time,
  • geography,
  • and economic activity.

The table shows figures according to time and geography, for a given value of activity. You can switch between activity panels by clicking on the two arrows at the left of the report:

Each dimension contains a hierarchy (higher levels group lower levels). The arrow signs in the table allow you to navigate through the hierarchies. Value of the turnover index are automatically agregated (using sum function) when you go to higher level in the hierarchies. Next version of the software will also provide average value, and you will be able to switch display between the two measures.

The colors inside the table wary according to the value of the measure. This is to allow you to spot higher and lower values easily. For example, at the default level, for Publishing activities, it is easy to see that Cyprus, Romania and Slovenia's turnover is growing more than the other countries' since 2008. Spain, Greece, Ireland, Lithuania and Latvia have lower turnover since 2009.

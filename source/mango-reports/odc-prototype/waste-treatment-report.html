Activity
The combination of actions that result in a certain set of products. An activity can be said to take place when resources such as equipment, labour, manufacturing techniques or products are combined, leading to specific goods or services. Thus, an activity is characterised by an input of resources, a production process and an output of products.
Hazardous waste
Hazardous waste refers to the categories of waste to be controlled according to national legislation on hazardous waste or the Basle Convention on the control of transboundary movements of hazardous waste and their disposal (Article 1 and Annex I).
Waste
Waste refers to materials that are not prime products (i.e. products produced for the market) for which the generator has no further use for own purpose of production, transformation or consumption, and which he discards, or intends or is required to discard. Waste may be generated during the extraction of raw materials during the processing of raw materials to intermediate and final products, during the consumption of final products, and during any other human activity.

Excluded in this definition are:

  • Residuals directly recycled or reused at the place of generation (i.e. establishment);
  • Waste materials that are directly discharged into ambient water or air.

Updates

This document shows most recent figures available in Eurostat on the 1st of June 2011.

Waste statistics for year 2010 are expected to appear in 2012 in Eurostat.

Data sources

The following datasets have been combined in order to produce the displayed figures:
  • Waste generation statistics have been extracted from Eurostat generation of waste dataset - bulk download facility - (env_wasgen). This dataset is described in the metadata document of Eurostat.
  • Waste treatment statistics have been extracted from Eurostat treatment of waste dataset - bulk download facility - (env_wastrt). This dataset is described in the metadata document of Eurostat.
  • Population statistics have been extracted from Eurostat demographic balance and crude rates dataset - bulk download facility - (demo_gind). This dataset is described in the metadata document of Eurostat.
  • Definitions of terms Activity, waste, hazardous waste have been extracted from RAMON, Eurostat's metadata server.

Data processing

Following filtering and grouping tasks have been performed in order to kept the volume of data presented reasonable:
  • Countries have been filtered (only EU27 countries have been kept).
  • Activities have been grouped according to Eurostat NACE R2 classification. The grouping presented in this document is similar to the top-level available in eurostat website.
  • Type of waste is grouped into categories defined by the European Institutions. This report shows only top-level categories.
  • No time series are presented because of the limited availability of dataset in time (only 2004, 2006 and 2008 are available).
  • Countries have been grouped into regions according to the United Nations geoscheme.

Total quantity of waste for EU27 - rounded values are provided by Eurostat - is not used in this report. Instead, detailed data of the Member states is automatically aggregated.

Cyprus has been re-assigned to Southern Europe region instead of Western Asia according to the United Nations country grouping.

Data quality

Quality of the statistics is documented: read 11.2 Quality documentation and 12. Quality management.

This software is a prototype and figures are presented for demonstration purpose only. We cannot guarantee that the figures displayed are absolutely correct. If you need to perform data analysis for professional purpose then we recommend to use other tools until we can provide you with a stable version. follow-us on twitter or contact us to stay updated.

This report displays one measure: quantity of waste treated per capita, according to three dimensions:

  • waste treatment,
  • geography,
  • and hazard factor.

The table shows figures according to treatment and geography, for a given value of hazard factor. You can switch between hazard panels by clicking on the two arrows at the left of the report:

Each dimension contains a hierarchy (higher levels group lower levels). The arrow signs in the table allow you to navigate through the hierarchies. Value of the quantity measure are automatically agregated (using sum function) when you go to higher level in the hierarchies.

The colors inside the table wary according to the value of the measure. This is to allow you to spot higher and lower values easily. For example, at the default level, for hazardous waste, it is easy to see that it is Deposit and recovery (other than energy recovery) are the most popular treatments. Northern Europe is treating a much higher quantity than the other regions.
